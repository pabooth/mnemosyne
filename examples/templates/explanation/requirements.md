# Document title

## Background

The situation that gives rise to these requirements, and who they serve.

## Goals

What success looks like, stated as outcomes.

## Non-goals

What is explicitly out of scope, so it cannot creep back in silently.

## Functional requirements

What the system must do. Number each requirement, state its priority
(e.g. MoSCoW), and give the rationale where it is not obvious.

## Non-functional requirements

The qualities the system must have: performance, availability, security,
operability, compliance. Make each measurable where possible.

## Constraints

The fixed points the solution must live within — technology, budget,
regulation, deadlines.

## Assumptions

What is assumed true; each assumption is a risk if it turns out false.

## Open questions

What remains undecided, and who owns getting each question answered.
